Recycl.AI classes explained

Recycl.AI is trained to recongise three main classes; Deposit recycling, Regualar recycling, and Flexible plastic. These classes contain most of the common items that are often not disposed off properly.

Deposit recycle

Deposit recycling is a system where consumers given a small deposit fee when for returning certain beverage containers at select recycle depos. Click here for at BC Return It locations

At the recycle depo, the returned containers are collected, sorted, cleaned, and processed to separate the materials for recycling. For example, aluminum cans may be melted down and used to make new cans, while plastic bottles may be shredded and transformed into new plastic products. The recovered materials are then sold to manufacturers who use them to make a wide range of goods. Deposit recycling encourages the proper disposal of beverage containers, reduces litter, and promotes resource conservation by ensuring that valuable materials are recycled and reused. Regular Recycle

Regular plastic, also known as rigid plastic, is range of plastic materials such as bottles, containers, and packaging that maintain their shape and structure. Often made from polyethylene terephthalate, polypropylene, or high-density polyethylene.

When regular plastic is recycled, it undergoes a series of steps to transform it into new products. First, the plastic is collected and sorted based on its type and grade. Then, it is cleaned and shredded into small pieces or pellets, which are then melted down and formed into new items through processes like injection molding or extrusion. Recycled plastic can be used to manufacture a variety of products, including furniture, automotive parts, and construction materials. Recycling regular plastic helps greatly reduces the amount of trash going to landfills. Click here for a list of accepted plastics

Flexible Plastics

Flexible plastic is a type of plastic that can be easily bent or folded without breaking. This category includes materials like plastic bags, shrink wrap, candy wrappers and flexible packaging

When flexible plastic is recycled, it undergoes a process where it is collected, sorted, cleaned, and melted into pellets. The pellets which can then be used to manufacture a variety of items, such as new bags, containers, or even synthetic clothes. In British Columbia flexible plastics can be recycled at recycle depos throughout the province. Click here for a list of accepted flexible plastics
